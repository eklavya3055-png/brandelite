Why Most Coaches Fail at Meta Ads
(And How to Fix It)

You've set up the campaign, hit publish, watched the budget drain — and got nothing. No calls booked, no leads converted, maybe a handful of clicks that went nowhere. Here are the 7 real reasons coaches fail at Meta Ads, and exactly what to do about each one.

📅 June 2025 ⏱ 7 min read ✍️ Brand Elite

Meta Ads — Facebook and Instagram advertising — are one of the most powerful client acquisition tools available to coaches today. The targeting is unmatched. The reach is global. The cost per lead, when the system works, is extraordinary.

But here's the reality: most coaches run Meta Ads wrong. Not because they're not smart enough — but because nobody told them that running an ad is only 10% of the job. The other 90% is everything that happens after someone clicks.

We've audited dozens of coaching ad accounts. The same mistakes show up every single time. Here are all seven of them.

Reason #1

You're Targeting Everyone — So You're Reaching No One

The most common mistake. Coaches set their audience to "people interested in personal development" aged 25–55 in five countries. That's not a target — that's the entire internet.

Meta's algorithm needs specificity to find the right people. When your audience is too broad, the algorithm has no signal to optimise against. It burns your budget testing millions of people instead of finding the hundred who actually want what you offer.

✦ The Fix

Niche down hard. Target one specific type of coach or consultant, one specific pain point, one specific country. "Female business coaches in the UK earning under £5k/month who want to scale online" will outperform "coaches interested in growth" every single time. Narrow audience + strong message = low cost per lead.

Reason #2

You're Sending Traffic to a Weak Landing Page

Your ad stops someone mid-scroll. They click. They land on your homepage — which talks about your entire business, your services, your story, your mission. They get confused and leave in 8 seconds.

A homepage is not a landing page. A landing page has one job: convert the visitor into a lead. When you send paid traffic to a page that does ten things at once, you get zero results.

✦ The Fix

Build a dedicated landing page for every ad campaign. One headline. One offer. One button. Remove the navigation bar so there's nowhere else to go. Your conversion rate will jump immediately. Even a simple Carrd or a single-section page beats a full website for ad traffic.

Reason #3

There Is No Follow-Up System

This is the biggest one. A lead fills in your form at 11pm on a Tuesday. You see it Wednesday morning. You send a message Wednesday afternoon. They've already spoken to three other coaches.

Speed to lead is everything. Studies show that responding within 5 minutes increases conversion by over 400% compared to responding within 30 minutes. Most coaches respond within 24 hours. That lead is gone.

✦ The Fix

Automate your follow-up. The moment someone submits a lead form, they should receive an instant WhatsApp or Instagram DM — personalised, warm, and pointing them toward booking a call. Tools like Manychat and Make.com make this possible in under 48 hours of setup. Every lead gets followed up in under 60 seconds, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Reason #4

You're Testing Too Many Things at Once

Five ad creatives. Three different audiences. Two different offers. Different copy on each one. You're spending £500 and getting £10 of data on each variation — which tells you nothing.

Meta's algorithm needs volume to optimise. When you split your budget across too many variables, no single ad gets enough data to learn from. The algorithm never exits the learning phase. Results stay terrible.

✦ The Fix

Start with one audience, one offer, and two ad creatives. Let the algorithm spend at least £20–£30 per day on each variation for 7 days before drawing any conclusions. Once you have a winner, scale that. Then test the next variable. One change at a time, always.

Reason #5

Your Offer Is Unclear or Too High-Commitment

"Book a discovery call to learn about my 6-month coaching programme" is a high-commitment ask from someone who has never heard of you before. Cold traffic doesn't convert on big asks.

The person seeing your ad doesn't know you. They don't trust you yet. Asking them to commit 60 minutes of their time and potentially thousands of pounds is too much friction at this stage.

✦ The Fix

Lower the commitment of your first ask. Offer a free audit, a free resource, a free 15-minute call — something that feels like obvious value with almost no risk. Once they're in your ecosystem and trust is established, the larger ask becomes easy. Think of it as dating before marriage.

Reason #6

You're Running the Wrong Campaign Objective

Coaches often run Traffic or Reach campaigns because they sound right. More traffic, more reach — that's what you want, right? Wrong. These objectives tell Meta to find people who click or scroll — not people who take action.

Meta's algorithm is extraordinarily good at finding the type of person you tell it to find. If you optimise for traffic, it finds clickers. If you optimise for leads, it finds people who fill in forms. The objective you choose shapes everything.

✦ The Fix

Use the Leads campaign objective with Meta's native lead forms, or the Conversions objective if you have a landing page with a properly installed Meta Pixel. Never use Traffic or Reach for client acquisition. Ever.

Reason #7

You Quit Before the Algorithm Learns

Day 3. You've spent £60. Zero leads. You turn the ads off. This is the most expensive mistake of all — because you just paid £60 for the algorithm to start learning, then threw that data away.

Meta Ads go through a Learning Phase — typically the first 50 conversion events. During this phase results are unpredictable, costs are high, and performance is inconsistent. Most coaches quit here, convinced ads don't work. The coaches who stay the course see costs drop significantly once the algorithm exits learning.

✦ The Fix

Commit to at least 2–3 weeks and 50+ conversion events before judging performance. Set a daily budget you can afford to spend for 21 days and don't touch the campaign during that time. Constant edits reset the learning phase. Patience at this stage is a competitive advantage — because your competitors are quitting at day 3.

The Real Problem: Ads Without a System

Here's the truth that ties all seven mistakes together: Meta Ads are not a lead generation strategy. They're the top of a system.

The coaches who win with Meta Ads aren't necessarily running better ads than you. They have a better system behind the ads. When someone clicks, there's an automated sequence that captures the lead, follows up instantly, qualifies them over WhatsApp, and books them directly into a calendar — all without the coach lifting a finger.

The ad is 10% of the equation. The system behind it is 90%.

When you fix the system, even average ads produce extraordinary results. When the system is broken, even great ads drain your budget with nothing to show for it.

Your Meta Ads Fix Checklist

  • Target one specific niche audience in one specific country
  • Send ad traffic to a dedicated landing page — not your homepage
  • Set up automated WhatsApp or DM follow-up within 60 seconds of every lead
  • Test one audience and two creatives — nothing more to start
  • Use a low-commitment offer as your first ask (free audit, free call, free resource)
  • Use the Leads or Conversions campaign objective — never Traffic
  • Commit to 21 days and 50 conversion events before evaluating performance
  • Install the Meta Pixel correctly on every page of your website

What Happens When You Get It Right

When all seven of these are fixed and a proper automation system is in place, Meta Ads become one of the most predictable, scalable client acquisition tools available. You know roughly what a lead costs. You know your close rate. You can do the maths and dial up the budget when you need more clients.

That's when coaching becomes a real business — not a hustle.
